Enterprise MDM Transformation

The mobile fleet had grown into a patchwork of 20,000 COPE and BYOD devices spread across rail operations, dispatching centers, workshops, and corporate offices. Policies varied between teams, security baselines were inconsistent, and provisioning a single device took well over 40 minutes of hands-on work. With NIS2 and ISO 27001 compliance deadlines approaching, the organization needed a ground-up rethink of its mobile device strategy.

I led the architecture and rollout, working closely with security, operations, and external vendors to land on enrollment models that actually fit each user group rather than forcing everyone into the same mold.

Enrollment strategy

Different roles have fundamentally different needs, so we designed four enrollment tracks instead of one:

  • COSU — single-purpose, kiosk-style devices locked to a specific set of apps. Used in workshops for maintenance tooling, in realtime dispatching centers for traffic control, and as urgent swap devices that train managers and drivers can grab and go when their primary device fails.
  • COBO — fully managed corporate devices for operational staff. Complete control over the device, no personal use. Hardened baselines enforced from enrollment.
  • COPE — corporate-owned devices with a personal profile for white collar workers. Work data stays containerized and policy-managed while users keep a personal side for their own apps.
  • MAM — app-level protection without device enrollment. Conditional access and data loss prevention policies applied directly to managed apps for users on personal devices who only need access to email, Teams, or a handful of corporate apps.

Security hardening

We built a unified set of security baselines through Intune and Knox Service Plugin, mapped to NIS2 and ISO 27001 controls. Device compliance policies, configuration profiles, conditional access rules, and app protection policies were standardized across the entire fleet. Devices that drifted out of compliance got flagged automatically and blocked from corporate resources until remediated. No more one-off exceptions or shadow configurations that quietly fell through the cracks.

Defender for Endpoint

Rolled out Microsoft Defender for Endpoint across all managed devices. Threat detection, EDR capabilities, and vulnerability management all integrated into the Intune stack. Combined with risk-based conditional access, a device that picks up a threat gets its access revoked in near real-time without waiting on a helpdesk ticket.

Provisioning & delivery

Onboarding was one of the biggest pain points. The old process involved manual configuration, multiple handoffs, and well over 40 minutes of hands-on time per device for the end user. We replaced it with fully automated provisioning through Intune staging profiles coordinated with external logistics vendors. Devices now ship pre-configured and ready to use out of the box. White glove delivery handles the edge cases. User setup time dropped to under 5 minutes.

Knox migration & fleet intelligence

The Samsung devices were managed through Knox Configure, which had its own licensing model and lifecycle separate from Intune. We migrated everything to Knox Service Plugin, consolidating Samsung-specific policies into the same Intune management plane as the rest of the fleet. On top of that, we rolled out E-FOTA (Enterprise Firmware Over The Air) for controlled OS updates across the Samsung fleet, and Knox Asset Intelligence (KAI) for real-time battery health monitoring, app usage analytics, and device lifecycle insights.

Alongside the technical migration, we audited the full device lifecycle from procurement to retirement, cleaned up unused licenses, and eliminated redundant tooling. Net result: more than €200k in annual savings.

Process overhaul

The technology changes only work if the processes around them change too. We reworked the end-to-end mobile device lifecycle: how devices get requested, approved, provisioned, shipped, swapped, repaired, and retired. Helpdesk workflows were redesigned, escalation paths clarified, and documentation rewritten so that frontline support could handle the most common scenarios without escalation. Vendor coordination for logistics, repairs, and returns was streamlined into defined SLAs with clear handoff points.

Impact

  • 20,000 devices migrated across COSU, COBO, COPE, and MAM
  • NIS2 and ISO 27001 compliance through enforced, auditable baselines
  • Defender for Endpoint deployed fleet-wide with risk-based conditional access
  • User setup under 5 minutes — down from well over 40
  • Zero-touch provisioning through staging profiles and vendor coordination
  • Knox Configure to KSP migration with E-FOTA and KAI fleet-wide
  • Lifecycle processes overhauled from request through retirement
  • €200k+ annual savings from license and tooling consolidation
