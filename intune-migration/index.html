Intune COBO Migration & Security Hardening

The existing mobile fleet was a patchwork of COPE and BYOD enrollments that had grown organically over the years. Different teams ran different policies, security baselines varied wildly, and onboarding a single device took nearly 40 minutes of manual work. With NIS2 and ISO 27001 compliance deadlines approaching, the organization needed a complete overhaul of its mobile device strategy.

I owned the end-to-end redesign: from enrollment architecture and security baselines to provisioning automation and vendor coordination. A fleet of 20,000 devices across rail operations, dispatching, workshops, and corporate offices, each user group getting the management model that matched their actual workflow instead of a one-size-fits-all policy.

Enrollment Strategy

COSU for single-purpose devices in workshops, realtime dispatching centers, and as urgent swap devices for train managers and drivers. Locked to specific apps, no user interaction needed beyond the task at hand. COBO for operational staff who need a fully managed corporate device. COPE for white collar workers who get a work profile alongside personal use. MAM for scenarios where app-level protection and conditional access are enough without enrolling the device itself.

Security Hardening

Built standardized security baselines through Intune and Knox Service Plugin, aligned to NIS2 and ISO 27001. Consistent policies across the entire fleet: device compliance rules, configuration profiles, conditional access policies, and app protection. No more exceptions or one-off configurations drifting out of compliance.

Defender for Endpoint

Rolled out Microsoft Defender for Endpoint across the entire managed fleet. Threat detection, EDR, vulnerability management, and risk-based conditional access all feeding into a single pane of glass. Devices that fall out of compliance get flagged and blocked automatically.

Provisioning & Delivery

Designed fully automated and white glove provisioning flows using staging profiles coordinated with external logistics vendors. Devices arrive pre-configured and ready to use. What used to take 40 minutes of manual setup now takes under 5 minutes with zero IT intervention.

Knox Migration

Migrated from Knox Configure to Knox Service Plugin, consolidating Samsung device management into the Intune stack. Cleaned up legacy licensing, optimized the device lifecycle from procurement to retirement, and cut more than €200k in annual costs.

Impact

  • 20,000 devices migrated across COSU, COBO, COPE, and MAM
  • NIS2 and ISO 27001 compliance through enforced, auditable security baselines
  • Defender for Endpoint deployed fleet-wide with risk-based conditional access
  • 87% faster onboarding — from 40 minutes down to under 5
  • Zero-touch provisioning through staging profiles and external vendor coordination
  • Knox Configure to KSP migration with consolidated device lifecycle management
  • €200k+ annual savings from license cleanup and lifecycle optimization
