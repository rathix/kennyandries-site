Homelab

This is where I break things on purpose. I use my homelab to test ideas, try out tools before recommending them, and generally mess around with infrastructure in ways I can't do at work. It's overkill for home use, but that's the point.

Compute

Everything runs on a Proxmox VE cluster. High availability, live migration between nodes, ZFS for storage. I can take a node offline for maintenance without anything going down.

Storage

Two TrueNAS SCALE boxes with ZFS. Snapshots, replication, separate pools for different workloads. They feed into Proxmox as shared storage.

Backups

Proxmox Backup Server running both on-site and at a remote location. Deduplicated, encrypted, incremental. If something goes wrong I can restore VMs and containers quickly.

Networking

MikroTik gear for routing and switching. VLANs to segment traffic, firewall rules between zones. Remote access through NetBird (Zero Trust) and site-to-site VPN. Reverse proxy with HTTPS for anything exposed. Self-hosted identity provider for SSO.

Services

Some of the stuff I run:

  • Nextcloud for files, calendar, contacts
  • Jellyfin for media
  • Vaultwarden for passwords
  • Pocket ID for SSO
  • Home Assistant for home automation
  • Immich for photo backups
  • Servarr stack for media management

Why bother

Mostly because I enjoy it. But it's also useful. I get to test things before suggesting them to clients, practice disaster recovery when nothing is actually on fire, and figure out what works and what doesn't without any pressure.
